\section{Evaluation and validation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

This section reports the extent to which the system has been validated. It separates what has been
\emph{verified on real hardware}, what has been \emph{build- or smoke-verified}, and what has
\emph{not yet been measured}, and it sets out the methodology for the measurements that remain. The
intent is to be precise about the system's maturity rather than to imply results that have not been
collected.

\subsection{Validation status}

The full pipeline, record a behaviour, label it, train models in the cloud, deliver them over the air,
classify on the collar, and display the result, has been exercised end to end on real hardware: the
collar reports an on-device class on its serial console and the relayed result reaches the dashboard.
\Cref{tab:validation} summarises the status of each capability.

\subsection{Resource footprint}

The collar is the binding resource constraint, and the firmware is built and reported against it. The
tensor arenas are statically allocated and are the dominant RAM cost of inference; the audio window
buffer and the BLE stack make up most of the rest. Enabling inference initially pushed RAM into the
mid-90\,\% range; eliminating the float-window scratch buffer (quantising directly from int16,
\cref{sec:on-device-ai}) brought it down into the low-to-mid 80\,\% band of the 256\,KiB part, which is
where the current build sits. Flash use is well under half the application partition.

\subsection{What is not yet measured}

The system has been validated for \emph{function}, not yet for \emph{performance}. The following
metrics are not reported here because they have not been collected on a representative dataset or over
a representative runtime; they are listed with the method by which each should be obtained, so the gap
is explicit and the measurements are reproducible.

\begin{table}[ht]
  \centering
  \caption{Pending measurements and the method for each.}
  \label{tab:pending}
  \begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}lX@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Metric} & \textbf{Method} \\
    \midrule
    Classification accuracy & Hold-out split of the labelled clips; report overall accuracy and a
      per-class confusion matrix for each cascade stage. \\
    Cascade benefit & Compare IMU-only accuracy with the gated IMU+audio cascade on the ambiguous
      eat/drink pair; report how often, and how correctly, the audio stage fires. \\
    Inference latency & Time \texttt{clf\_classify} on-device across many windows; report mean and
      worst case for each stage. \\
    Battery life & Run the collar to cutoff under a representative behaviour mix; report hours, and
      the sensitivity to the audio-stage duty cycle. \\
    Power draw & Bench-measure current at the cell in each state (idle, IMU inference, audio inference,
      streaming, OTA). \\
    OTA transfer time & Time a full model push over BLE; report bytes/s and total time per slot. \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Threats to validity}

Three caveats bound the current results. First, the behaviour data used so far is limited, so reported
function does not yet imply field accuracy; a two-class model in particular will confidently label an
idle cat as one of its two classes until an explicit idle/other class is added
(\cref{sec:future-work}). Second, the demo dashboard is populated partly by a simulator
(\cref{sec:cloud-backend}), which is realistic but synthetic and must not be read as measured
performance. Third, validation to date is single-cat and within-subject; multi-cat households are out
of current scope. These are tracked as future work and do not affect the parts marked verified in
\cref{tab:validation}.
