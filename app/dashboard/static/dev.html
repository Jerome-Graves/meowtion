Mode

Training (default): the station records and uploads labelled clips , use the toggles below to collect data. Production: the station stops recording and uploading entirely and just relays the collar's on-device eat/drink/purr classification. Switch to Production once your models are trained and flashed.

Actions to recognise

The behaviours your collar learns , eat, drink, purr, scratch, litter-tray use… anything you can capture and tell apart. These fill the label dropdown on each clip, and the trainer learns whatever set you define here.

Train models (cloud)

Trains the eat/drink/purr models from your labelled clips on the server (no local setup) and stores them on Firebase. Run it after you've collected and labelled a batch.

Data capture

When ON, the station records audio clips back-to-back whenever a registered collar is in range. Turn it OFF when you're done collecting so it stops recording. (Training mode only , ignored in Production.)

Manual capture (ignore range)

Records whenever a registered collar is heard at any distance, ignoring the range gate. Use this to capture purring, which happens while the cat is resting or being petted, not at the bowl. Works on its own, independent of the toggle above. Turn it OFF when you're done.

Capture range

How close the cat must be (signal strength) before the station connects and records. Stronger / less-negative = must be closer. Applies to all your stations. Measure the real value with the cat at the bowl, then set the threshold a few dB below it.

Recorded clips

Audio clips the station captured at the bowl. Each row shows its label, length and whether it has paired IMU data. Set the action (eat / drink / purr) from the dropdown, or click a row to expand it and load the waveform: drag the start / end markers to cut dead space, Play to preview, then Save trim. The trim is a non-destructive window, applied to the audio and the time-aligned IMU together. Delete the clips that aren't good for training. Only expanded clips load their audio, so a long list stays fast.
